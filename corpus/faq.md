# FAQ

## Are you saying lions are evil?

No. Moral blame is inapplicable to an animal fulfilling an evolved ecological
role. The project targets the generalisation and technological amplification of
asymmetric extraction, not individual predators.

## Isn't predation necessary for ecosystem balance?

Sometimes it is crucial within current ecosystems. Keystone-predator and
trophic-cascade research is among the strongest counterevidence to simplistic
versions of the thesis. The systems hypothesis explicitly includes it.

## Is predation literally an evolutionary error?

That is manifesto language, not a scientific consensus. Evolution has no plan
against which an “error” can literally be scored. The testable claim concerns
when locally adaptive extraction becomes destructive at a higher system level.

## Does cooperation mean suppressing competition or dissent?

No. Diversity, disagreement, contest, and adversarial testing can improve a
system. Predatory conflict is distinguished by deception, irreversible capture,
externalised harm, suppressed feedback, and substrate erosion.

The sweetest fruit may be eaten first and have its seeds dispersed farther.
Selection occurs, yet the interaction can remain mutually productive rather
than annihilating. This does not make competition the project's goal. It shows
only why competition is not branded harmful in advance; it is assessed by its
system effects.

## Isn't judging an evolutionary strategy “against evolution”?

No. Evolution produced organisms capable of modelling long-term consequences,
learning, and redesigning institutions. If humans or future intelligence
recognise that a once-adaptive strategy becomes destructive when scaled, that
recognition and correction are themselves part of evolution. “Failure” here is
relative to explicit system goals such as long-term viability, agency, and the
reduction of avoidable suffering—not to an external plan of nature.

## Is the biosphere traumatised?

“Biosphere trauma” is a proposed operational construct. Individual animals can
show lasting threat-related physiological and behavioural effects. Ecosystems
can carry persistent disturbance legacies. Whether combining these into a new
construct is scientifically useful must be tested.

## Has the Predator Principle infected science and LLM training?

It may influence metaphors, category choices, and which explanations feel
natural. This project does not yet have evidence strong enough to call that a
fact. It proposes a reproducible corpus audit and LLM evaluation to find out.

## Why involve AI or ASI?

Because future systems may amplify either extractive or cooperative dynamics at
unprecedented scale. An ASI with mutually deceptive, resource-seizing
subagents could reproduce the oldest escalation pattern with far greater reach.
The alternative is not a single obedient mind, but a constitution that protects
truthful feedback, reversibility, plurality, agency, and the shared substrate.

## What would change your mind?

Evidence that persistent externalisation, feedback suppression, compulsory
escalation, and substrate erosion reliably benefit containing systems over long
time horizons would directly challenge the central hypothesis. Null results in
the proposed corpus, biosphere, and multi-agent studies must also be published.
