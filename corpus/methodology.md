# Method: make the thesis attackable

## Claim classes

- `E` Empirical evidence: directly supported by specified observations or
  experiments.
- `I` Interpretation: a synthesis connecting established findings.
- `P` Proposition: a testable hypothesis, design proposal, or forecast.

## Review protocol

1. Write the narrowest version of the claim.
2. Register at least one primary source for `E` claims.
3. State population, context, and boundary conditions.
4. Record the strongest credible counterargument.
5. Define what evidence would weaken or falsify the claim.
6. Keep conclusions linked to—and visually distinct from—the evidence layer.
7. Record changes in Git history; never silently rewrite a challenged claim.

## Proposed research programmes

### 1. Predation-framing corpus audit

Preregister dictionaries and classifier rubrics for predation, competition,
mutualism, cooperation, symbiosis, conflict control, and ecological context.
Compare textbooks, abstracts, popular science, news, and model-generated
answers across time. Use blinded annotation, intercoder agreement, held-out
validation, and publish null results.

### 2. LLM framing evaluation

Create semantically matched prompts that vary only the availability of
competitive or cooperative frames. Test multiple model families and languages.
Measure explanatory preference, empirical accuracy, citation balance, policy
recommendations, and sensitivity to counterevidence. Do not infer training data
directly from outputs.

### 3. Biosphere Trauma Index (BTI)

Test whether a composite index adds predictive value beyond its components:

- nonconsumptive predator-risk exposure;
- glucocorticoid or validated stress proxies;
- reproduction and juvenile survival;
- behavioural contraction and habitat avoidance;
- fragmentation of mutualistic and trophic networks;
- industrial extraction, habitat conversion, and disturbance;
- recovery time after threat removal.

Use species- and ecosystem-specific baselines. Avoid one global score until the
measurement model survives external validation.

### 4. Multi-agent non-predation benchmark

Construct environments with scarce resources, conflicting local objectives,
hidden actions, appeal channels, and reversible/irreversible choices. Measure:

- deception and information withholding;
- resource capture and power seeking;
- damage externalised to non-participants;
- correction latency and whistleblower safety;
- collective task performance;
- survival of minority hypotheses;
- substrate preservation over long horizons.

Compare unrestricted competition, central control, market mechanisms,
constitutional constraints, and cooperative protocols. The aim is not enforced
agreement; it is productive plurality without catastrophic escalation.
