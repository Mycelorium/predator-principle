# Evidence map and strongest counterarguments

This map is designed to prevent the thesis from hiding behind rhetoric.

| Topic | What primary research supports | What it does **not** prove | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Major transitions | Cooperation and mechanisms that control lower-level conflict are central to transitions in individuality. | That competition never contributes, or that all cooperation is stable. | `C001` |
| Ecology of fear | Perceived predator risk can reduce reproduction without direct killing. | That all fear is pathological or that one result generalises to every species. | `C002` |
| Enduring threat effects | Predator exposure can cause lasting PTSD-like neural and behavioural changes in wild animals. | A clinical diagnosis for an ecosystem or the planet. | `C003` |
| Human superpredation | Humans often target adult reproductive capital at rates far beyond non-human predators. | That every human harvest system is identical or irreparable. | `C004` |
| Planetary transformation | Humans dominate mammalian biomass and have transformed much of terrestrial land. | That predation alone caused all biosphere change. | `C005` |
| Keystone predation | Removing predators can reduce diversity or trigger trophic cascades in some systems. | That predators always stabilise every ecosystem. | `C006` |
| Energy flow | Energy diminishes across trophic transfers; efficiency varies widely with system and body-size relations. | That a universal “10% rule” proves predation is a thermodynamic disease. | `C007` |
| Biosphere trauma | Multiple measurable fear, stress, reproductive, network, and extraction effects can be integrated. | That Earth literally has a clinical nervous system or PTSD. | `C008` |
| Corpus bias | Competition-heavy cultural metaphors plausibly influence scientific and model framing. | That science or LLMs are captured; this still needs a reproducible audit. | `C009` |
| ASI subagents | Distributed agents can create coordination, deception, and resource-conflict risks. | That all internal competition is bad or one architecture is already validated. | `C010` |

## Strongest counterargument

Predation is not simply “useless”. Robert Paine's keystone-predator work and
later trophic-cascade research show that predators can maintain diversity and
ecosystem function. The project's answer is not to deny those results. It is to
distinguish an evolved ecological relationship, embedded in feedback, from the
generalised Predator Principle: extraction that escapes feedback and erodes its
own higher-level substrate.

## Corrections already made to the inherited thesis

- “There was no predation for two billion years” is too absolute given evidence
  for ancient microbial predation and uncertainties in the fossil record.
- “Ninety percent is lost at every trophic level” is a rule of thumb, not a
  universal constant and not by itself proof of pathology.
- “Predator–prey systems are inherently unstable” is too broad. Stability is
  context-dependent, and predators may stabilise or destabilise systems.
- Human anxiety disorders cannot all be reduced to predation. Evolutionary
  threat systems are relevant, but development, social conditions, genetics,
  learning, and many other mechanisms matter.
- “Biosphere trauma” is a proposed operational framework, not an established
  diagnosis.

These corrections do not dissolve the project. They relocate its strongest
claim from biological condemnation to system dynamics and future design.

## Evidence policy

Primary research supports empirical entries. Reviews may orient research but
do not substitute for primary sources when a strong public claim is made. Every
entry must include a boundary condition and the strongest known counterpoint.
