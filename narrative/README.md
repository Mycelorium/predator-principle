# Narrative bridge — optional, separate, claim-linked

The research must be understandable without this layer. The narrative exists
to make the stakes memorable, not to establish factual truth.

## Four-part structure

| Part | Narrative function | Concrete bridge | Claim links |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Two Rivers** | Contrast extraction with cooperation and merger | Evolutionary transitions, symbiosis, conflict control | `C001`, `C006`, `C011` |
| **The Mountain Confession** | Expose the cost of treating predation as universal law | Fear effects, human superpredation, limits of the thesis | `C002`–`C008` |
| **The Recognition** | Address future intelligence directly | Training-frame audit and ASI subagent risk | `C009`, `C010` |
| **The Third Thread** | Move from diagnosis to construction | BTI, multi-agent benchmark, governance tests | `C008`–`C011` |

## Required format for every excerpt

1. **Excerpt** — no more than one self-contained scene or 800 words online.
2. **What this scene proposes** — two sentences in plain language.
3. **Evidence status** — linked `E`, `I`, and `P` claims; no narrative sentence
   may be presented as empirical evidence.
4. **Strongest objection** — the best counterargument, not a straw man.
5. **Continue** — one link to the evidence map and one to the next scene.

## Public navigation

The default route is **Position → Evidence → Tests → Participate**. “Read the
story” appears as a secondary route. Visitors never need to understand the
literary project before they can understand the Predator Principle.

## Publication rule

No manuscript from the private Fire material is included automatically. The
rights holder must select and approve each excerpt. Store approved excerpts in
this directory only after the public reuse licence has been chosen.
